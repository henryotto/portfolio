Responsive Web Design (RWD)

Responsive web design is now an interesting topic for most web developers to understand. It has been going through tough trials over the years until today that it has come to be a prerequisite in web development. Responsive web design or RWD for short is basically the baseline of screen sizes.

RWD solves different problems to how end users view content on the web. More companies and industries design and develop large desktops to small phones and because of RWD, we cannot see the difference when viewing information on such devices because of its capability to scale down sites to fit different screen sizes.

There is a wide variety of information on responsive web design available all over the Internet, and much of that information is theoretical and unreliable. RWD always comes down to three techniques that all work in conjunction and they are Fluid Images, Fluid Grids, Media Queries.
